    New Job as a Research Assistant

    Navigating life as an international student turned out to be more challenging than I anticipated. The initial excitement overshadowed the reality that I wouldn't be returning home for the next four years; I needed to transform my new environment into a home. Securing employment became crucial for self-financing, and it was imperative to find a source of enjoyment to alleviate the stress, worry, and homesickness that inevitably crept in. Transitioning from a tourism-focused student to delving into Computer Science, my initial inclination was towards becoming a tour guide – a role that promised variety, outdoor engagement, networking opportunities, and unique connections.

    However, my eagerness to align my work with my academic pursuits led me to explore new opportunities. I saw a research assistant role for Computer Science. My mindset was I’ll inquire and see if I am fit. I did so, and on the spot I was offered the role. I won’t lie I didn’t know what I would “research”, but to my surprise it was exactly what I was looking for. As a research assistant in Computer Science, my responsibilities involved updating an app, implementing changes, maintaining an up-to-date database, and even proposing new features. It was what I needed to learn concepts hands-on whilst learning in class.Undoubtedly, it presented challenges, and there were moments of frustration, because it showed me how much I don’t know. However, one thing I know is that I am wiser now, knowing the things I don’t know.
